## Why Core CS Subjects Matter üìö

**Google might skip it, but almost every other company from startups to FAANG will grill you on core computer science subjects: SQL, OOPS, OS, Networks, DBMS.**

### **These aren't just theoretical subjects - they can be deal breakers**

**Companies don't want you to format definitions. They want you to understand things and explain why things need to be done this way and not the other way.**

### **Real Interview Examples**:
- **How would you use indexes in SQL?**
- **How would you avoid deadlocks in operating systems?**
- **Why would you prefer UDP over TCP in a gaming app?**

### **Why These Rounds Matter**:
- **Measures depth of knowledge**
- **Tests practical understanding**
- **Reveals foundation strength**
- **Even if you're very good at DSA, weak foundations can break your interview**

## Subject-Wise Key Areas üéØ

### **1. SQL - Database Queries** üíæ

#### **Why First Priority**:
- **A lot of companies ask database-related queries**
- **Directly applicable in day-to-day work**
- **Builds foundation for DBMS concepts**

#### **Key Areas to Focus**:
- **Query writing** (SELECT, JOIN, SUBQUERY)
- **Indexes** and optimization
- **Transactions** and ACID properties
- **Database normalization**
- **Connection to DBMS theory**

#### **Common Interview Questions**:
- **Find second highest salary** in employee database
- **Complex JOIN operations** with multiple tables
- **Subquery optimization**
- **Index usage** and performance

#### **Critical Points**:
- **Practice actually writing SQL queries**
- **Cannot write queries in test without practice**
- **Understand query optimization** techniques
- **Know how SQL connects to DBMS theory**

---

### **2. OOP + Low-Level Design (LLD)** üèóÔ∏è

#### **Why High Priority**:
- **Interviewers love asking OOP questions these days**
- **Tests ability to structure real-world problems**
- **Essential for machine coding/L L D rounds**

#### **Key Concepts to Master**:
- **Polymorphism** (runtime and compile-time)
- **Inheritance** and hierarchies
- **SOLID principles**
- **Abstraction** and **Encapsulation**
- **Design patterns**

#### **Common Interview Formats**:
- **Write C++/Java code with OOP concepts embedded**
- **Real-world scenario questions** (design parking lot)
- **Machine coding rounds**
- **Object-oriented modeling of problems**

#### **Must-Prepare Problems**:
1. **Parking Lot Design**
2. **Elevator System Design**
3. **Book My Show (seat booking system)**
4. **At least these 3 problems before any interview**

#### **Critical Points**:
- **Cannot write object-oriented code directly**
- **Implement classes on your own before interview**
- **Focus on SOLID principles and design patterns**
- **Practice structuring real-world problems into code**

---

### **3. Operating Systems** ‚öôÔ∏è

#### **Why Medium Priority**:
- **Fundamental concepts used in coding**
- **Tests understanding of system-level programming**
- **Interviewers go deep into these topics**

#### **4 Key Topics to Master**:

##### **A. Processes & Threads**
- **Differences between process and thread**
- **Process states** and transitions
- **Thread synchronization**
- **Inter-process communication**

##### **B. CPU Scheduling**
- **Scheduling algorithms** (FCFS, SJF, Round Robin)
- **Process scheduling** criteria
- **Real-time scheduling**

##### **C. Deadlocks**
- **Deadlock conditions** (mutual exclusion, hold and wait, etc.)
- **Deadlock prevention** and avoidance
- **Deadlock detection** and recovery

##### **D. Memory Management**
- **Paging and segmentation**
- **Virtual memory**
- **Memory allocation** strategies
- **Page replacement algorithms**

#### **Common Interview Questions**:
- **Thread vs Process differences**
- **Producer-consumer problem**
- **Deadlock prevention strategies**
- **Virtual memory concept**

#### **Critical Points**:
- **Interviewers try to go deep into these topics**
- **These are things used in actual code**
- **Clarity of OS concepts really matters**

---

### **4. Database Management Systems (DBMS)** üóÑÔ∏è

#### **Why After SQL**:
- **Builds upon SQL knowledge**
- **Provides theoretical foundation**
- **Ties practical queries to database theory**

#### **Key Areas to Focus**:

##### **A. Transactions & ACID Properties**
- **Atomicity, Consistency, Isolation, Durability**
- **Transaction states** and management
- **Concurrency control**

##### **B. Indexing & Query Optimization**
- **Types of indexes** (B-tree, Hash, etc.)
- **Query optimization** techniques
- **Index usage patterns**

##### **C. Normalization**
- **1NF, 2NF, 3NF, BCNF**
- **Normalization principles**
- **Denormalization trade-offs**

##### **D. ER Diagrams**
- **Entity-Relationship modeling**
- **Relationship types** (1:1, 1:M, M:N)
- **Database design** principles

#### **Common Interview Questions**:
- **What is a deadlock in database?**
- **Explain ACID properties with examples**
- **Normalization form questions**
- **Index selection and optimization**

#### **Critical Points**:
- **Very frequent topic in service-based companies**
- **Definitely prepare databases very well**
- **Connect theory with practical SQL knowledge**

---

### **5. Computer Networks** üåê

#### **Why Lower Priority (Optional)**:
- **I sometimes term it as optional**
- **Not often required in many interviews**
- **Basic concepts usually sufficient**

#### **When to Prepare in Detail**:
- **Applying for network-specific roles** (Oracle network roles)
- **Company specializes in networking products**
- **Job description emphasizes networking**

#### **Basic Topics to Cover**:
- **TCP vs UDP differences**
- **HTTP vs HTTPS**
- **OSI or TCP/IP layers**
- **DNS and CDN systems**
- **How browser works** when searching domain

#### **Common Interview Questions**:
- **Difference between TCP and UDP**
- **What happens when you type google.com**
- **HTTP vs HTTPS security**
- **Network layers and their functions**
- **Difference between 2G, 3G, 4G**

#### **Critical Points**:
- **Going into too much depth is not often required**
- **Prepare basics unless applying for network roles**
- **Real-world scenarios are commonly asked**

## Study Strategy & Tips üìñ

### **Structured Preparation Approach**:

#### **Phase 1: Foundation Building**
1. **SQL** - Query writing and optimization
2. **OOP Concepts** - Core principles and implementation
3. **Basic OS concepts** - Processes, threads, scheduling

#### **Phase 2: Advanced Topics**
1. **LLD** - Design patterns and system design
2. **DBMS Theory** - Transactions, indexing, normalization
3. **OS Deep Dive** - Deadlocks, memory management

#### **Phase 3: Specialized Topics**
1. **Networks** - Based on target companies
2. **Advanced DBMS** - Query optimization, distributed databases
3. **System Design** - If targeting senior roles

### **Common Mistakes to Avoid** ‚ùå

#### **1. Blind Preparation**
- **Don't waste months preparing everything equally**
- **Focus on high-yield topics first**
- **Prioritize based on target companies**

#### **2. Theory-Only Approach**
- **Don't just memorize definitions**
- **Understand practical applications**
- **Practice implementation and problem-solving**

#### **3. Skipping Practice**
- **Cannot write SQL queries without practice**
- **Cannot write OOP code directly in interviews**
- **Practice coding problems for each subject**

#### **4. Ignoring Company-Specific Needs**
- **Research target companies' interview patterns**
- **Adjust preparation based on job requirements**
- **Focus on relevant topics**

### **Success Metrics üìä**

#### **For Each Subject, You Should Be Able To**:
- **Explain concepts clearly and concisely**
- **Provide practical examples**
- **Answer follow-up questions**
- **Solve related problems**
- **Connect theory to real-world applications**

#### **Interview Readiness Checklist**:
‚úÖ **SQL**: Can write complex queries with joins and subqueries
‚úÖ **OOP**: Can design classes and apply SOLID principles
‚úÖ **OS**: Can explain processes, threads, and deadlocks
‚úÖ **DBMS**: Understand ACID, indexing, and normalization
‚úÖ **Networks**: Know TCP/IP stack and basic protocols
