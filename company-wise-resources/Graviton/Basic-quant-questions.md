# Basic Quant Questions

## Puzzles:

### 1) Water and Wine Mixture
There are two beakers, one containing water, the other wine (equal volumes). A certain amount of water is transferred to the wine, then the same amount of the mixture is transferred back to the water. Is there now more water in the wine than there is wine in the water?

### 2) Tigers and Sheep Problem
Hundred tigers and one sheep are put on a magic island that only has grass. Tigers can live on grass, but they want to eat sheep. If a Tiger bites the Sheep, then it will become a sheep itself. If 2 tigers attack a sheep, only the first tiger to bite converts into a sheep. Tigers don't mind being a sheep, but they have a risk of getting eaten by another tiger. All tigers are intelligent and want to survive. Will the sheep survive?

### 3) Sock Drawer Problem
Imagine yourself as a busy IIT student who has a drawer filled with 5 red socks, 10 green socks, and 15 blue socks. How many socks do you need to pull out of the drawer to guarantee that you have a pair of socks in the same color?

### 4) Light Bulb and Switches
There is a light bulb inside a room and four switches outside. All switches are currently at off state and only one switch controls the light bulb. You may turn any number of switches on or off any number of times you want. How many times do you need to go into the room to figure out which switch controls the light bulb?

### 5) Counterfeit Coins Problem
You have 10 bags of 100 coins, and in all of them except for one, every coin weighs exactly 10 grams. However, in the counterfeit bag, all coins weigh either 9 or 11 grams. You need to identify the counterfeit bag in just one weighing, and you have a digital scale that provides accurate weights. Can you successfully find the counterfeit bag in just one attempt?

## Basic Maths

### 6) Zero in Numbers
How many positive integers at most 10000 have the number 0 somewhere?

### 7) Handshake Problem
At a wedding of 100 people, each person shakes hands with every other person once. How many handshakes occur in total?

### 8) Geometric Progression Sum
Derive the formula for calculating the sum of a Geometric progression

## Probability

### 9) All Faces on Die
We roll a 6-sided die n times. What is the probability that all faces have appeared?

### 10) Increasing Order Dice
We throw 3 dice one by one. What is the probability that we obtain 3 points in strictly increasing order?

### 11) Monty Hall Problem
Suppose you're on a game show, and you're given the choice of three doors: Behind one door is a car; behind the others, goats. You pick a door, say No. 1, and the host, who knows what's behind the doors, opens another door, say No. 3, which has a goat. He then says to you, "Do you want to pick door No. 2?". What is the probability to win car if you switch?

### 12) Russian Roulette Probability
Two bullets are put into a gun's round barrel consecutively, which has a capacity of 6. The gun is shot once, but no bullet is fired. Does rolling the barrel (shuffling) before the next shot increase the probability of firing a bullet?

### 13) Truth Teller Probability
A man is known to speak the truth 3 out of 4 times. He throws a die and reports that it is a six. Find the probability that it is actually a six.

## Expectation

### 14) Expected Coin Tosses for Heads
On average, how many times must a fair coin be tossed to get a heads?

### 15) Expected Tosses for HT Pattern
Find the expected number of tosses of a fair coin to get HT for the first time.

### 16) Expected Die Rolls for Six
On average, how many times must a 6-sided die be rolled to get a 6?

### 17) Expected Rolls for Consecutive Sixes
On average, how many times must a 6-sided die be rolled until two 6s appear consecutively?

### 18) Letters and Envelopes Problem
There are n letters and n envelopes. Your servant puts the letters randomly in the envelopes so that each letter is in one envelope and all envelopes have exactly one letter. Calculate the expected number of envelopes with the correct letter inside them.
